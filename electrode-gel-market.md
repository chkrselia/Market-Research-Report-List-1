<p><p>The electrode gel market is divided into two main types: halide-containing gels and non-halide gels. Halide-containing gels typically include salts that enhance conductivity and are commonly used in medical and diagnostic applications. In contrast, non-halide gels are formulated without these salts and often aim to minimize skin irritation, making them preferable for sensitive skin or extended use. Both types serve essential roles in ensuring optimal electrode performance and patient comfort in various applications, including electrocardiograms and electrotherapy.</p></p>
