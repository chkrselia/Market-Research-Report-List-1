<p><p>The foot orthoses market is segmented into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to enhance movement and provide support during activities, catering to athletes and individuals with active lifestyles. They allow for flexibility and adaptation to various foot movements. In contrast, static orthotics focus on maintaining foot alignment and providing stability, often used for conditions like flat feet or overpronation. Both types aim to alleviate pain and improve foot function but serve different needs based on activity levels.</p></p>
