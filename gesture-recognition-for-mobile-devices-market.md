<p><p>Gesture recognition for mobile devices is categorized into two main types: 2D and 3D gesture recognition. 2D gesture recognition involves detecting hand movements or touches on a two-dimensional plane, primarily using touchscreens, enabling intuitive control through swipes and taps. In contrast, 3D gesture recognition captures movements in three-dimensional space, often utilizing sensors or cameras to interpret gestures like waving or pointing. Both technologies enhance user interaction, offering seamless navigation and control in various applications, including gaming, communication, and smart home devices.</p></p>
