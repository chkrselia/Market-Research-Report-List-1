<p><ul><li>Pig Source</li><li>Human Source</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The medical fibrin sealant market is categorized into pig source and human source types based on the origin of the fibrinogen and thrombin components used in the sealants. Pig source fibrin sealants are derived from porcine blood and are often favored for their abundant availability and effectiveness in promoting wound healing. In contrast, human source fibrin sealants are obtained from human plasma, ensuring biocompatibility and reducing the risk of zoonotic infections but may have higher processing costs. Both types are crucial in surgical applications for hemostasis and tissue adhesion.</p></p>
