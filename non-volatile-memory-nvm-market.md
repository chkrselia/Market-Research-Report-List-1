<p><p>Non-Volatile Memory (NVM) retains data without power, and its market is divided into electrically addressed and mechanically addressed types. Electrically addressed NVM includes technologies like flash memory and EEPROM, which use electrical signals to read and write data. Mechanically addressed NVM, such as magnetic tape and hard drives, relies on physical movement to access and store information. Both types serve diverse applications, with electrically addressed solutions favoring speed and efficiency, while mechanically addressed options offer large capacity and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
