<p><p>Telecoms and broadband markets encompass various technologies for internet access, including Cable, DSL, FTTP, Wi-Fi, and WiMAX. Cable broadband uses existing television cable infrastructure, offering high-speed internet services. DSL leverages telephone lines for broadband connectivity, suitable for urban areas. FTTP delivers fiber-optic connections directly to homes, providing superior speeds. Wi-Fi facilitates wireless local area networking, allowing devices to connect to the internet without physical cables. WiMAX, a fixed wireless technology, extends broadband access to rural or underserved regions.</p></p>
