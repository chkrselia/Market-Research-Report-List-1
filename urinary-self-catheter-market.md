<p><p>The urinary self-catheter market includes three main types: Foley catheters, intermittent catheters, and external catheters. Foley catheters are indwelling devices that remain in place for extended periods, typically used in hospitals or for patients with chronic conditions. Intermittent catheters are used on a scheduled basis, allowing for greater independence and comfort for users. External catheters, often designed for men, are applied externally to collect urine, providing a non-invasive option. Each type caters to specific needs in urinary care.</p></p>
